# THE VERDICT PLAYTEST — family session guide

The question this session answers: **does this game have real potential —
enough to polish, monetize, and sell?** Everything below is designed to get
a clean answer in ~45 minutes of play.

## Setup (2 minutes)
- Hard-refresh the game (Ctrl+Shift+R) — kawaii art is ON by default now.
- First visit loads ~900 images in the background; art may "pop in" during
  the first minute. That's expected (and cached forever after).
- One save file per kid + one for dad. Turn Little Piper ON for the
  youngest (title menu).
- Click once anywhere so the sound + announcer wake up.

## Session plan
1. **Free play first (15 min).** No instructions beyond "pick your
   character and animal friends." WATCH, don't coach. The tutorial and
   announcer should carry them — note every place they don't.
2. **The bunny test.** Does your daughter find and pick bunnies on her
   own? Does she smile at the kawaii art? (This is the art verdict.)
3. **Character swap (10 min).** Have them try a second bandleader.
   Do they *feel* the difference (BAM's triple-send, VIVI's fast recall,
   ECHO's booms)?
4. **Dad plays Riverside/Farm (10 min)** while kids watch — do they cheer
   for robot-in-pond sizzles and lightning strikes? Spectator fun counts.
5. **The one-more-run test (the verdict itself).** When you say "last
   game," what happens? Negotiation = YES. Shrug = polish more. Walked
   away earlier = investigate why.

## Questions to ask (after, not during)
- "Which animal is your favorite? Why?"
- "What was the scariest robot?" (Want: an answer with a grin, not fear.)
- "What do you want to buy next?" (Tests if the acorn economy landed.)
- "Show me your favorite thing in the game." (Whatever they demo IS the
  core product.)
- For the older kid: "What would you change?"

## What to write down
- Every moment of confusion (what were they trying to do?)
- Every laugh (what triggered it?)
- Every death: did THEY know why? (loss legibility is our law)
- Anything they ignored completely (quests? spices? training camp?)
- The exact words of the verdict conversation.

## Known rough edges (don't let these color the verdict)
- Music is placeholder synth (Suno upgrade is gated on the verdict).
- Announcer voice is robotic Web Speech (same gate; can be turned off in
  pause menu if it grates).
- Art "pops in" on the very first load only.
- Arena backgrounds are still simple tints — props/backgrounds polish is
  the next art pass.

Verdict lands in CLAUDE.md afterward, and steers everything next.
